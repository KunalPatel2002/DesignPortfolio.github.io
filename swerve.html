SWERVE DRIVETRAIN FOR FRC TEAM 846:

This Swerve Drivetrain that I designed features four of these modules mounted to the frame of our robot. Because the wheels are steered by a motor, it means the robot can travel omnidirectionally. Additionally, it means that the robot can spin while moving in a straight line, which is useful for evading defense.

DRIVING SYSTEM:



Detailed view of Driving System(Steering components and upper plate removed)



Due to the nature of the swerve module, at least one gear had to be at the center of the steering axis, so that the distances between gears was always the same no matter what angle the wheel was pointed at. I designed it so that the pinion gear of the motor was at the center of this axis. Power goes from the motor, to the yellow gear through an intermediate gear, to a set of bevel gears which ultimately drives the wheel.
